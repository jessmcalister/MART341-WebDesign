The Animals

Of Glacier National Park

GNP is home to 71 different species of animals. These range from big preditors to small prey. We will be going over five different species.

Grizzly Bear

The grizzly bear is one of the biggest preditors at Glacier National Park, eating animals from small rodents to moose. Even though they are at the top of the food chain, much of their diet consists of nuts, berries, fruit, leaves, and roots. A female can weigh anywhere from 290-440 pounds and a male generally weighs around 600 pounds. According to a DNA study, there's roughly 300 grizzly bears living within GNP.

Elk

An elk can weigh up to 500 pounds and mainly spend their meals eating green grasses. Rut occurs in late September and early October and will have their calves in late May or June. A newborn calf will weigh around 30 pounds. Elk roam the meadows of Glacier National Park and cna be seen in the lower vallies.

Mountain Goats

The mountain goat is arguably one of the most popular animals to see in Glacier National Park. Males can weigh up to 260 pounds while females will range from 130-200 pounds. They will usually eat lichens, ferns, grasses, herbs, shrubs, and deciduous or coniferous trees. To have the best chance of seeing one of the magnificent animals, take to the Sperry Trail and continue all along the area east of Gunsight Pass.

Mountain Lion

The mountain lion goes by many different names such as cougar, panther, puma, painter, el leon, and catamount. GNP is prime mountain lion habitat and will hunt deer, elk, moose, and bighorn sheep. On average, a female mountain will weigh 140 pounds and a male will weigh 220 pounds.

Deer

Glacier National Park is home to both Mule Deer and White-Tailed Deer. The difference between the two is that Mule Deer has large ears while White-Tailed Deer have brown faces and white undersides that flip up when they run. They will eat brose, fobs, mast, and grass.
